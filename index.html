The Dussehra

Dussehra, also known as Vijayadashami, is a major Hindu festival that celebrates the victory of good over evil. It is celebrated on the tenth day of the month of Ashvina, which is the seventh month of the Hindu calendar. The festival marks the triumph of Rama over the demon king Ravana, who abducted Rama's wife Sita.

Dussehra is celebrated in different ways across India, but some common ways include:

  • Ram Lila: A theatrical enactment of the story of the Ramayana in North India
  • Burning effigies: Large effigies of the demon king Ravana, often along with his son and brother, are set on fire with crackers
  • Processions: Families gather for processions
  • Dressing up: Children dress up as characters from the Ramayana
  • Sweets: Families enjoy sweets together
  • Harvest: Farmers begin harvesting Kharif crops like rice, cotton, and maize
  • Exchanging leaves: In Maharashtra, some communities exchange leaves from the Apta tree as a symbol of gold
  • Immersion: In eastern regions of the Indian subcontinent, the Durga image is immersed in a river
